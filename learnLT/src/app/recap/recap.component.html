Accentuation

Every Lithuanian word contains a stressed vowel or a dipthong, the stress of which can either be short or long. In case of a short stress, the vowel is marked with a grave accent (kairinis) ' ` '. If it is a long stressed vowel, it is additionally classified as either one that has rising pitch (marked by a circumflex (riestinis) ' ˜ '), or one that has falling pitch (marked by an acute accent (dešininis) ' ´ ').

Lithuanian accentuation is for the most part not constant, which means that the same noun can have different syllables stressed in different ways depending on its inflectionand number. Which syllable the stress will shift to can be determined if you know which accentuation paradigm a noun belongs to. There are 4 accentuation paradigms, which divide nouns based on their stress in the dative plural and accusative plural cases.
