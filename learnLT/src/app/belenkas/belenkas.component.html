In Modern Lithuanian there are 5 declension classes, which can be determined by noun endings in the nominative singular and the dative plural.

1st declension

Masculine nouns that end by -as and -ias in nominative singular, -ams and -iams in dative plural

vyras, vyrams (man)

2nd declension

Feminine nouns that end by -a and -ė in nominative singular, -oms and -ėms in dative plural

Pay attention to the genitive plural case: if a noun's stem ends with a palatalised consonants "d" or "t", they become "dž" or "č" respectively. Palatalisation is marked by the letter "i".
