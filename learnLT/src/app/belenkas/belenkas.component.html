This declension also requires special attention to the genitive plural ending -ų: some nouns have this ending after a hard consonant (e.g. ausis -> ausų ('ear'), šaknis -> šaknų ('root')), others after a palatalised one (e.g. ugnis -> ugnių ('fire'); nosis -> nosių ('nose')). It is best to memorise the genitive plural ending of each feminine 3rd declension noun, as the ending difference is due to historical reasons, and the nouns no longer contain any visible cues for the correct genitive ending.

Nominative / Vardininkas

Primarily used to express the grammatical subject of the sentence:
